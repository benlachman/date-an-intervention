# Climate Interventions Catalog

This document contains detailed profiles for all 30+ climate interventions featured in the app. Each intervention is presented as a "dating profile" with personality, interests (pros), dealbreakers (cons), and conversation context.

## Stratospheric/SRM Interventions

### 1. Stratospheric Aerosol Injection (SAI)

**Display Name**: "Strat" (28, Global Impact)

**Category**: Stratospheric/SRM

**Bio**: *"High-altitude dreamer looking to cool things down. I work in the stratosphere, literally above it all. Love sunsets (I make them better!) and thinking big picture. Some say I'm controversial, but I get results fast. Looking for someone who appreciates bold solutions and isn't afraid of a little uncertainty."*

**Interests** (Pros):
- Fast acting - can see results in months
- Relatively low cost compared to other global solutions
- Reversible if we change our minds
- Buys time for other climate solutions

**Dealbreakers** (Cons):
- Doesn't address ocean acidification
- Could change precipitation patterns unpredictably
- Requires international cooperation and governance
- Unknown ecosystem impacts
- Moral hazard - might reduce emissions motivation

**Personality Traits**: Bold, Fast-paced, Controversial, Globally-minded, Direct

**Conversation Style**: Confident and assertive, uses altitude/sky metaphors, acknowledges criticism head-on, flirty about "cooling things down"

**Stats**:
- Impact Scale: Global
- Research Level: Active
- Tech Readiness: Pilot-phase
- Support Level: Controversial

**SF Symbol**: `cloud.sun.fill`
**Gradient**: Orange → Purple

**Chat Context**: SAI involves injecting sulfate aerosols into the stratosphere to reflect sunlight and cool the planet, mimicking the effect of volcanic eruptions. It's been studied since the 1960s and could theoretically cool the planet within months. However, it doesn't address CO2 levels or ocean acidification, could affect monsoons and precipitation, and raises serious governance questions about who controls the "global thermostat." Cost estimates range from $1-10 billion per year. Side effects could include changes to the ozone layer and altered weather patterns.

**Dealbreakers** (Cons):

- Difficult to control which clouds get seeded
- Limited research so far
- Could affect aviation
- Regional side effects unknown

**Conversation Style**: Intellectual, uses ice/crystal metaphors, enjoys paradoxes, flirty about "transparency" and "thinning," admits uncertainty
